Lisa Su

A transformative leader who revitalized AMD and revolutionized the semiconductor industry

A timeline of Lisa Su:

  • 1969 - Born in Tainan, Taiwan, and immigrated to the United States as a child.
  • 1991 - Graduated from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) with a Bachelor of Science degree in electrical engineering.
  • 1994 - Completed her Ph.D. at MIT, specializing in semiconductor devices.
  • 1995 - Joined IBM as a research staff member and eventually became the Vice President of Semiconductor Research and Development.
  • 2007 - Moved to Freescale Semiconductor as the Chief Technology Officer.
  • 2012 - Joined AMD as Senior Vice President and General Manager of Global Business Units.
  • 2014 - Appointed as AMD's President and CEO, becoming the first woman to lead a major semiconductor company.
  • 2016 - Released AMD's Ryzen processors, marking a turning point for the company and challenging Intel's dominance.
  • 2020 - Announced AMD's acquisition of Xilinx for $35 billion, further solidifying the company's position in the semiconductor industry.
  • 2021 - Named one of the world's greatest leaders by Fortune and credited with turning AMD into a top competitor in the chip industry.
