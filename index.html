Lisa Su

A transformative leader who revitalized AMD and became a driving force in the semiconductor industry.

Key Moments in Lisa Su's Career

  • 1969: Born in Tainan, Taiwan; later immigrated to the U.S.
  • 1994: Earned a Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from MIT, focusing on semiconductor research.
  • 1995–2007: Led cutting-edge semiconductor innovations at IBM, rising to VP of R&D.
  • 2012: Joined AMD as Senior VP, leading global business operations.
  • 2014: Appointed President & CEO of AMD — the first woman to lead a major chipmaker.
  • 2017: Spearheaded the launch of Ryzen and EPYC processors, reestablishing AMD’s market position.
  • 2020: Announced AMD’s $35B acquisition of Xilinx, expanding into data centers and AI.
  • 2021: Recognized by Fortune as one of the world’s greatest leaders for her industry-shifting impact.
